Using Upteer with your website

This is a technical document that assumes you are familiar with HTML and CSS. If that's not you, the short story is that any opportunities you list on Upteer can be easily displayed on your own website.

Displaying your opportunities

After you have set up your organization on Upteer, you can also display opportunities on your own site simply by adding the following HTML to one of your web pages: 

<div id="upteerdisplaydiv">12345</div>
<script src="https://www.upteer.com/js/embed.js"></script>

Replace the 12345 in the example with the ID of your organization. You can get your organization ID by "right clicking" the link to your organization from your profile. The upteerdisplay div can be anywhere you want the opportunities to be displayed, as long as it comes before the embed script.

Those two lines are all you need to display your current opportunities and allow volunteers to interact with them. The rest of this document is additional options if you want them.

Making the opportunities display match your site

By default, the opportunities displayed on your site look just like they do on Upteer. If you want the opportunities to match the look and feel of your own site, you can override the default style for everything in the display by providing your own css file: 

<div id="upteercssoverride">https://yoursite.org/css/upteerlookandfeel.css</div>

Replace the CSS file URL with the complete link to the CSS file on your site. Upteer will include your CSS file last when it generates the content display, so you can override anything you want. If you run into any difficulty customizing any display elements, let us know. Our goal is to make this as easy as possible.

Automatic login for your users

If volunteers do not already sign in to your website, Upteer will handle sign in as needed and you can just skip this section. If you have volunteers who sign in to your site, and you would like to automatically log them in to Upteer to interact with your opportunities there, add the following HTML above the embed script on your web page: 

<div class="upteeruseremail">name@example.com</div>
<div class="upteerusername">Profile Name</div>
<div class="upteeruserpic">https://yoursite.org/pics/profpic.png</div>

The first div is required. The second two are optional, but Upteer may prompt the user to complete their profile if they are not available. The divs can be anywhere on the page before the embed script. If you don't want this information to show on the page, you can set the style of the divs to display:none. Either way, only the person logged in will see their own information.

For security, the page embedding your Upteer opportunities must be on the same site as the home page for your organization as listed on Upteer.

When the user interacts with Upteer through your site, Upteer first checks to see if they have an Upteer account using their email address. If no account is found, then Upteer creates a profile using the information provided and sets it to be accessible via your web site. If the user has a profile accessible from your site, then they are automatically logged in and interact with the listed opportunities the same way as if they had logged into Upteer directly. No need to log in a second time. If the user already has a profile, but it is not yet accessible from your site, then they are prompted to add access permission for your site before continuing.
